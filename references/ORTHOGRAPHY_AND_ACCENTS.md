# Orthography, Tonos, and Punctuation / Ορθογραφία, Τόνος και Στίξη

## Modern Greek Accent: Tonos

Modern Greek normally marks stress with one tonos on words of two or more syllables:

- **άνθρωπος**, **παράθυρο**, **καλημέρα**
- Monosyllables usually have no tonos: **το**, **με**, **σε**, **και**

Important exceptions and contrastive monosyllables:

- **πού** and **πώς** take tonos in direct or indirect questions: **Πού πας;**, **Δεν ξέρω πού είναι.**
- **που** without tonos is usually a relative/complementizer: **το βιβλίο που διάβασα**.
- **ή** means “or”; **η** is the feminine definite article.

## One-Tonos Rule

A word normally has only one tonos. In combinations where a weak pronoun follows and pushes stress rhythmically, an extra accent can appear in careful orthography:

- **δώσε μου** but **δώσε μού το** may appear when needed to avoid ambiguity.
- **ο δάσκαλός μου** often keeps a second accent in the noun phrase because the possessive clitic follows a proparoxytone noun.

Avoid overusing double accents. Use standard forms unless clarity requires the extra mark.

## Final Sigma

Use **ς** only at the end of a word:

- Correct: **κόσμος**, **της**, **ένας**
- Incorrect inside a word: **κόςμος** → **κόσμος**

Use **σ** elsewhere:

- **σπίτι**, **μεσαίος**, **θάλασσα**

## Diaeresis / Διαλυτικά

Use diaeresis when two vowels that usually form a digraph must be pronounced separately:

- **χαϊδεύω**, **λαϊκός**, **πρωινός**
- With stress on the first vowel, the tonos can make separation clear: **Μάιος**.

Common digraphs:

- **αι** = /e/ as in **παιδί**
- **ει, οι, υι** = /i/ as in **είμαι**, **οικογένεια**
- **ου** = /u/ as in **ουρανός**
- **αυ/ευ** vary by following sound: **αυγό**, **αυτό**, **ευχαριστώ**, **εύκολος**

## Capitalization

- Sentence starts and proper names are capitalized: **Η Μαρία μένει στην Αθήνα.**
- Days and months are normally lowercase unless at sentence start: **Δευτέρα**, but in running text **τη Δευτέρα**; **Ιανουάριος**, but **τον Ιανουάριο**.
- Nationality nouns/adjectives can be lowercase in ordinary usage: **Έλληνας**, **ελληνικός**. Proper names remain capitalized.

## Greek Punctuation

| Mark | Function | Example |
|---|---|---|
| `;` Greek question mark | question | **Πού είσαι;** |
| `·` ano teleia | semicolon-like pause | **Ήρθε αργά· δεν μίλησε.** |
| `,` comma | similar to English but often before subordinate clauses | **Όταν ήρθε, φύγαμε.** |
| `.` period | full stop | **Τελείωσε.** |

In informal digital writing, `?` often appears, but standard Greek uses `;` for questions.

## Tonos in Enclitic and Possessive Phrases

Possessive clitics follow nouns:

- **το βιβλίο μου**
- **η μητέρα μου**
- **ο δάσκαλός μου**

When the noun is stressed on the third syllable from the end, a second accent often appears before the clitic:

- **ο άνθρωπός μου**
- **το μάθημά μου**

## Common Homophone and Spelling Traps

| Trap | Distinction | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **η / ή** | article vs or | **η Μαρία ή ο Νίκος** |
| **που / πού** | relative/complementizer vs where | **το σπίτι που είδα**, **πού είναι;** |
| **πως / πώς** | that vs how | **μου είπε πως θα έρθει**, **πώς το ξέρεις;** |
| **ότι / ό,τι** | that vs whatever/anything that | **είπε ότι φεύγει**, **πάρε ό,τι θέλεις** |
| **με / μαι** endings | preposition vs verb ending | **με βλέπει**, **κοιμάμαι** |
| **ε / αι** endings | active vs passive/middle or plural distinctions | **παίζετε**, **παίζεται** |

## Apostrophe and Elision

Apostrophes mark omission in informal or set expressions:

- **σ’ αγαπώ** = σε αγαπώ
- **απ’ το σπίτι** = από το σπίτι
- **γι’ αυτό** = για αυτό

Avoid excessive elision in formal writing.

## Numbers and Agreement

- **ένας/μία/ένα** agrees in gender: **ένας άντρας**, **μία γυναίκα**, **ένα παιδί**.
- **τρεις** is masculine/feminine; **τρία** is neuter: **τρεις μέρες**, **τρεις άντρες**, **τρία παιδιά**.
- **τέσσερις** is masculine/feminine; **τέσσερα** is neuter.

## Correction Heuristics

When checking orthography:

1. Ensure every multisyllabic word has the correct tonos.
2. Check question words: **πού/πώς** vs **που/πως**.
3. Check final sigma.
4. Check common endings: **-ε/-αι**, **-ο/-ω**, **-ει/-η/-οι**.
5. Preserve informal style if the user’s text is casual, but mark formal alternatives.
